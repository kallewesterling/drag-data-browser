Jackie Maye

Comments

  • "who reigns supreme at the Club Calais, has just acquired a black velvet evening gown that looks like the money, and taking a tip from those who know, the girls around town would do well to go up and see how to wear dresses to bteter advantage than the season's leading debutantes." (Brooklyn Times Union, 10)
  • Star billing (Cleveland Lets Down Bars to Femme Imps, Variety, July 1, 1936, 38)
  • "The practical joke at the Club Calais is to get a chump to dance with 'Jackie' Maye, the femme impersonator" (Courier-Post, 14; The Post-Star, October 25, 1930, 4)
  • "At the Club Calais, Jackie Maye, the boy performer, told of a bitter experience he had with a friend. 'I loaned him $50 to buy a tuxedo. I didn't mind the money so much, but the guy doesn't come here any more.' Jackie was very sad. 'I'll never believe another man as long as I live,' he declared." (Daily News, 21)
  • Club Abbey's chorus girls, under the "master (?) of ceremonies" Jackie Maye. (Daily News, 80; Daily News, November 8, 1931, 6)
  • "Only Female Impersonator to play in MOTION PICTURES . . . Heading a new ALL-STAR cast of the leading Female Impersonators in the business." (maye-jackie-09-18-37)
  • "Frances Dunn and Arthur Budd are back at Torch Club, Police Commissioner Mulrooney giving them permission—but Jackie Maye hasn't gotten the official okay yet. It seems strange, but Broadway can't boast of one real hot stay-out-late spot where the crowd gathers." (New York Daily News, 20)
  • "Because one night at the Club Calais while Jackie Maye was doing his female impersonation, a certain newspaperman who wasn't wise to it proposed to Jackie." (New York Daily News, 21)
  • "The main novelty here is a lad named Jackie Maye, who is dressed in girl's clothing and looks better than many a lass. His favorite ditty is, 'I Must Have That Man.'" (New York Daily News, 22)
  • "Over at the Club Calais there is also a new spring edition featuring Frances Faye's piano thumping and Jackie Maye's new spring gowns." (New York Daily News, 31)
  • "has let his hair grow and now has a girlish bob . . . He's still better looking than any of the chorines there." (New York Daily News, 32)
  • "receives orchids almost nightly from a society lad." (New York Daily News, 36)
  • "At the Club Calais . . . 'I like that one very much,' he said pointing to Jackie Maye. 'That isn't a girl, that's a fellow,' I told him. 'Stop trying to kid me, Sid,' he said. 'I think I know a girl when I see one.' I tried to convince him that Jackie was a male wearing female clothing, but he wouldn't believe me. 'I'll bet it's really a girl,' he went on, 'and she tells you columnists she's a fellow so you'll think she's a good female impersonator." (New York Daily News, 53)
  • "Mulrooney okayed Jackie Maye for the Lido Club on condition Jackie wears a Dietrich tuxedo." (New York Daily News, 60)
  • "now singing 'straight'" (Night Club Notes, Miami Daily News, May 20, 1939)
  • "The success of Mallin [sic; Jean Malin] may or may not be responsible for the presence at the Club Calais of one Jackie Maye. As one who never went in for female impersonators with any great degree of applause still and all this Jackie Maye appears the most incredible of them all. I have yet to note a person who, without advance knowledge, saw through Maye's disguise." (Star-Gazette, 6)
  • "a female impersonator"; "scores a personal triumph for excellence in his particular field. In a well-modulated falsetto, he does Gone With the Wind, ending the number in a surprisingly clear high note. In a more mannish vein, he offers Copper-Colored Gal, capping it with a bit of Suzi-Q" (The Billboard, November 13, 1937, 22)
  • "Jackie Maye and Show Place Revue, headline the program [at the Bay Ridge Theatre]" (The Brooklyn Citizen, 8)
  • "later I hear that Jackie Maye plans to appear at the Club Lido, 52d St., despite Mulrooney's ban upon sissy performers... Maye is taking a hint from Marlene Dietrich and will work in trousers, hoping that he will evade the Mulrooney edict." (The Brooklyn Daily Eagle, 13)
  • "(both of 'em [Jean Malin and Jackie Maye] flaming femme impersonators), we can't help but wonder whatever happened to that police edict barring female impersonators from the local nite clubs—you know, the edict that got so much publicity immediately after the Club Abbey shooting." (The Brooklyn Daily Eagle, March 21, 1931, 9)
  • female impersonator from Club Ha-Ha, "wearing his $10,000 gown" (The Miami News, 27)
  • "Seen for the first time in Philadelphia . . . reputed 'tops' as a female impersonator" (The Philadelphia Inquirer, 17)
  • "female impersonator, has a good singing voice" (Variety, September 17, 1930, 54)
