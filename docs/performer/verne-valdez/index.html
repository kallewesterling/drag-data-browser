Verne Valdez

Comments

  • "the new emsee at the Club Piccadilly" (Club Talent, The Billboard, December 11, 1937, 24)
  • "Vern Valdez signs out of the Blackstone . . . and goes into Club Blase, that city" (Club Talent, The Billboard, January 22, 1938, 6)
  • "Comedy Singer and Dancer" (Detroit Free Press, November 20, 1942)
  • "black-faced . . . blues singer, in his Aunt Jemina specialty, furnishes some Tucker style vocalizing." (The Billboard 51, no. 24, June 17, 1939, 20 (C M. Litteljohn, "Night Club Reviews: Spinning Wheel, Seattle"))
  • Eighth month at the venue (The Billboard, Dec 5, 1942, 14)
  • "after six months at Club Piccadilly . . . now current at Club Blackstone" (The Billboard, Jan 1, 1938, 29)
  • "billed as the 'Male Fanny Brice,' is in his sixth week at the Ballyhoo, Columbus, O. (The Billboard, January 23, 1937, 13)
  • "after 10 weeks at Wagon Wheel . . . has moved to Finrcchio's [sic] . . . for indefinite run." (The Billboard, Jul 9, 1938, 16)
  • "appearing at the Wagon Wheel, Houston, after an eight-week run in Cleveland." (Last stop in Cleveland was at Club Blase, according to Club Talent, The Billboard, January 22, 1938, 6) (The Billboard, March 26, 1938, 21)
  • "is now completing his second month at the Wagon Wheel" (The Billboard, May 14, 1938, 18)
  • "has spent half a year here [at Club Piccadilly]" (The Billboard, Nov 6, 1937, 24)
  • "closed a six-week engagement at Finocchio's . . and located at Club Tivoli there" (The Billboard, November 19, 1938, 18)
  • "Current show includes" (The Billboard, October 26, 1935, 15)
  • "after 58 weeks at Frolic Inn, Hollywood, now current at Washington Club, East Liverpool, O." (The Billboard, October 31, 1936, 13)
