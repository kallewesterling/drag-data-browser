Sepia Gloria Swanson

Comments

  • Dead after a year of illness. Born in Atlanta, moved to Chicago at an early age. Said to have started "the vogue for adopting the names of noted women in the entertainment world. He was also said to have been one of the first to adopt feminine dress entirely, and was often seen in gorgeous evening gowns, costly furs, high heel shoes, etc." (Baltimore Afro-American, May 4, 1940, 14)
  • Legal name here is "Mr. Winston," full legal name corroborated by Afro-American, December 24, 1932, 18. (On 'Gloria Swanson' (Real Name: Mr. Winston), 221)
  • appeared with Bobbie Howell, female saxophonist (The Chicago Defender, December 31, 1932, 5)
  • Dead at 33 years old. (The Chicago Defender, May 4, 1940, 21)
  • "For years Gloria has been one of the outstanding floor show personalities and in his latest role as headliner at the Britwood, the impersonator's work is supreme." (The Chicago Defender, September 25, 1937, 11)
  • Dead. The column makes it sound like Swanson died in a train wreck but in actuality from disease. (The New York Age, May 4, 1940, 4)
